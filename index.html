Frequently Asked Questions
How many rights of nature are there?

As of 2026, we have tracked 683 eco jurisprudence initiatives in 63 countries and territories. 512 of them are rights of nature. Yes, there is a difference. Check out our initiative index to explore the beautiful diversity of the movement.

Wait, there is more than the rights of nature?

We track all kinds of ecocentric law around the world. Next to the rights of nature, we monitor eco-governance systems, indigenous models, personhood, animal rights, and local eco knowledge. Our codebook has all the definitions you need.

What else do you track?

For every initiative, we try to gather as much information as possible: who, what, when, where, how, why? That means a lot of boring and many more exciting rabbit holes. Have you ever heard about the disputed Indonesian province of West Papua? Well, now you did.

You do have some mistakes on the website!

We are doing our very best to be the most reliable database on global ecocentric law. We are also humans and make mistakes. If you find one or more, please let us know and we will fix them as quick as we can. An initiative is missing? Please tell us that too. We rely on our community. Thank you!

Can I use your data?

Yes, please do. All of our data is under a CC BY-SA 4.0 license and follows additional data ethics. That means you can use and adapt it for all the projects you want, even commercially. We only ask you to cite us properly and to continue the spirit of open knowledge. Let us know about what you do. We are always excited to hear about, collaborate on, promote, or support your project in any way we can.

Who pays for all of this?

We wouldn't be here without generous grants from the Rockefeller Brothers Fund, Environment Now, and countless hours of a small group of priceless academics, dedicated volunteers, topical and regional experts, motivated interns, and a luckily growing EJM community. You can also support us directly by donation.

Why are you doing this?

Because ecocentric law is inspiring! In fact, it is so inspiring that we want people to know all about it. We believe in rigorous research and superb science communication. We also believe that facts should inform opinions, not the other way around.

Speaking of science communication, your website is pretty nice! Who made it?

You are seeing the result of years of work, of frustration, and steep learning curves. You would not believe how difficult it is to make something look easy. We had amazing people doing amazing things and are very proud of how it turned out. Special thanks to our website 2.0 designer Louis and our data-viz-ard Carmen.

But you still use a Eurocentric world map? Ugh.

While designing the page, we thought about using many different projections that would reflect our core values: Dymaxion, South-up, Waterman Butterfly… you name them. Ask our cartographer-in-chief Alex about it. For all of our design choices, we were looking for the ideal combination between innovation and feasibility. Eventually, it always all boils down to accessibility in its many shapes and forms. Hey, at least it's not Mercator.

Ok, this all sounds very cool. Can I work for you?

Of course you can! We are always looking for new people that can help to make this project better. We don't really have the funds to pay you, but we are more than happy to support you in finding some. When in doubt, reach out!
